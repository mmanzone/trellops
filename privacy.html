Privacy Disclaimer

Transparency is our priority. Here is how we handle your data.

1. The "Local First" Philosophy

Trellops is designed as a client-side application. This means that, for the vast majority of features, your data never leaves your computer browser. We do not track your activity, sell your data, or store your Trello card contents on our servers.

2. Data Storage

We store your dashboard configuration (such as block names, tile colours, and layout settings) in your browser's Local Storage. This ensures your dashboard is ready whenever you open Trellops.

Exception: Sharing Configurations

If you explicitly choose to use the "Share Configuration via Link" feature, we must briefly store a "skeleton" of your layout on our secure server to make the link work for your colleagues. This data includes:

  • Trello Board IDs
  • Block Names and Layout order
  • Home Address coordinates (if set)

Note: We do not store any personal card data, card descriptions, or user personal information (PII) in this process.

3. Trello Permissions (Read vs. Write)

When you log in, Trellops requests access to your Trello boards. We strictly limit this access based on the features you use.

Default: Read-Only Access

By default, Trellops only requests READ permissions. We download your lists and cards solely to display them on your dashboard. We cannot modify, delete, or create data.

Optional: Write Access

If you wish to use advanced Map features, Trellops requires WRITE permissions. You will be prompted to authorise this only if you attempt to:

  1. Move a Card on the Map: Dragging a card from one location/list to another on the Map View.
  2. Save Coordinates: Updating a Trello card to permanently save the latitude/longitude decoded from its address (this speeds up future loading).

4. Third-Party Services

To provide the Map View features, Trellops uses the following third-party service:

  • Google Maps Platform: If your cards contain addresses in the description field (rather than coordinates), we send that address text to Google Maps to convert it into map coordinates (Geocoding). Please refer to the Google Privacy Policy for more details.

5. Contact

If you have questions about this policy or your data, please contact us via our support channels.
